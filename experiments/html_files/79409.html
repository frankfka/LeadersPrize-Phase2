Continuum hitting its stride

By Mary Louise Schumacher
July 3, 2008
  • Tweet
  • Email
  • Print
  • (0) Comments

The design for the new Milwaukee Job Corps Center campus, which will serve hundreds of disadvantaged students, has just been completed and released by Continuum Archictects + Planners.

Construction for the $27 million to $29 million project, tucked within 25 acres of the former Bacher Farms at N. 60th St. between Good Hope and Mill roads, is slated to begin in November and be completed in 2010.

The campus includes eight buildings, including a learning resource center, state-of-the-art cafeteria, a recreation facility and dorms. The green spaces were given as much attention at the structures, the firm says. The federally funded program will provide basic education, vocational training, work experience, job placement, counseling, healthcare and other services to students between the ages of 16 and 24 living in Wisconsin and surrounding states.

Continuum was chosen over 17 other firms by the U.S. Department of Labor for the design job. As part of the next phase, requests for construction bids for the 155,000 square foot campus will be posted to fedbizoppos.gov in mid-July.

Continuum, a 10-year-old Milwaukee-based architectural firm, is truly hitting its stride. In addition to the Job Corp center, it's also won contracts for the new Saint John's on the Lake high-rise tower on the East Side and the expansion of the Chazen Museum of Art in Madison. The combined budget of these three major projects is $100 million.

"Continuum takes a highly selective approach, which emphasizes quality projects that the firm has professional and personal interest in," said Ursula Twombley, the firm's lead architect on the Job Corps project, in a statement.
