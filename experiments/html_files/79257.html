'Afghan forces making strides,' says Marco Rubio during Afghanistan visit

U.S. Senator Marco Rubio, speaking with U.S. forces during a weekend visit to Kabul. (Photo provided by Rubio's office)

Speaking via phone during a weekend trip to Afghanistan, U.S. Senator Marco Rubio said Afghan forces were making strides as they train to take over from NATO forces.

"Some of the men in these forces have never driven a vehicle before," Rubio told reporters on a morning conference call. "There are some serious challenges."

Rubio joined six other Senators, including Mitch McConnell, the Minority Leader in the U.S. Senate, and Lindsey Graham of South Carolina in a four-day trip to the war-torn country and neighboring Pakistan. After meeting with General David Petraeus and dining with Afghan President Hamid Karzai, Rubio said he felt the tide was turning.

 

"Momentum of Taliban in the south has clearly been reversed," he said. "Everywhere we went here, what we heard repeatedly is that it was important that the U.S. is committed to seeing this through. People are afraid to buy into a governing system, they're afraid to vote or take jobs in the government because they think we're leaving in six months or a year. They've got to know we are committed."

 

Rubio reiterated his support for the 2014 withdrawal goal outlined by NATO officials at a Lisbon summit late last year.
