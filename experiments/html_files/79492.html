Homicides in 2009 -- fewer in number, shocking in range from mass shootings to series of domestic killings

Print Email
The Oregonian/OregonLive By The Oregonian/OregonLive The Oregonian
Follow on Twitter
on January 02, 2010 at 5:04 PM, updated January 03, 2010 at 7:50 AM
comments

The following list includes homicides and the status of the investigations. Solved means police have made an arrest or identified a suspect but doesn't reflect a conviction:

PORTLAND

View full sizeMackintosh familyKim Mackintosh with her husband and son. She was her father's caregiver.
1. Kim Mackintosh, 43. Shot in the head with a shotgun in her Southeast Portland home on Jan. 16. She died six days later, Jan. 22. Her 92-year-old father, John Wesley Myers, was taken into custody that day and accused of attempted aggravated murder in the shooting of her and her brother, Mark Myers, 49, who was shot in the stomach. The shooting occurred about 7:30 a.m. at the home in the 4200 block of Southeast Cora Street. Officers found Mark Myers wounded outside the home, and Kim Mackintosh on a couch inside, and ordered the elder Myers to come out of the house with his hands up. Mackintosh's 12-year-old son, who is severely autistic, was found hiding inside the home but was unharmed. A Remington shotgun was in the basement. The boy was placed in a temporary foster care home for developmentally disabled children. Mackintosh was described as her son's and father's full-time caregiver. Myers told officers at the scene he was upset about his bank account being depleted, and believed his daughter was trying to commit him to get control of his house. A judge found Myers, who has dementia, unfit for trial, dismissed murder charges and let him move into a Southwest Portland adult care home. SOLVED.

2. Robert Joseph Campbell, 64. Stabbed to death at 1:12 a.m. Jan. 23 in the Rosenbaum Plaza, 1218 S.W. Washington St., a Housing Authority-run apartment building. Police found Campbell in an apartment and Melvin Earl Parker, 60, in the building . He was accused of one count of murder. Both men were residents of the building, which is described as offering single-room living for the elderly and disabled. Their fight might have been over a woman. A grand jury chose not to indict Parker, and the state dismissed the charge. SOLVED.

Courtesy of Wilks familyAshley Wilks
3. Ashley Wilks, 16. Shot and killed about 10:30 p.m. Saturday, Jan. 24 as she stood in front of The Zone underage nightclub in downtown Portland. The suspect, Erik Salvador Ayala, 24, shot nine people and then shot himself. Wilks, a sophomore at Clackamas High School, was an athlete and honors student preparing to spend her junior year in France as part of the Rotary Exchange program. The family moved to Happy Valley from Colorado a couple of years ago. SOLVED.

4. Martha Paz De Noboa, 17. Shot in front of the Zone and died later at OHSU Hospital. DeNoboa, from the Peruvian city of Arequipa, had been staying with a White Salmon, Wash., family since September as a Rotary exchange student and was enrolled as a junior at White Salmon's Columbia High School. She was buried in her hometown Feb. 7 after a memorial service attended by about 300 people. De Noboa's mother, Martha Delgado Lozada, expressed gratitude to White Salmon and Rotary officials for their outpouring of sympathy and for making sure her daughter's body returned to Peru, he said. "Thankfully, she is home, and now she rests in heaven," De Noboa's mother said. SOLVED.

5. Sean Liam Kelly, 40. Stabbed to death April 14 on the front yard of a North Chase Avenue home. A Multnomah County grand jury indicted Ralph Anthony Williams, 54, in the stabbing. Kelly had obtained a temporary stalking order against Williams on March 18, citing harassment, intimidation, racial slurs and vandalism. He had a court date to seek a permanent stalking order. Both men lived in independent housing for the mentally ill in North Portland run by Cascadia Behavioral Healthcare. According to Williams, the day of the killing, Kelly had come into the common kitchen area, told Williams to get out of his way, and went back to his apartment. Williams told police he had "had enough" of Kelly, grabbed a butcher knife and waited for Kelly to return. Once he returned, Williams told police, he "let the knife do the talking." He said he stabbed Kelly five to seven times in the upper chest and head. As Kelly tried to run across the street, Williams tried to stab him in the back, a probable cause affidavit says. When the knife blade broke, Williams picked up a cinder block and struck Kelly in the face as he lay dying on neighbor's front yard. A trial is scheduled for May 2010. Williams told court authorities he suffered from PTSD, depression and had been prescribed medication, but quit taking them a year ago. Kelly died of stab wounds and blunt force trauma. SOLVED

Chance Domingo Perez (left) and his friend Andrew Gabriel Perez
6. Chance Domingo Caron, 18. Shot in the head by roommate Andrew Gabriel Perez,18, with a pistol-grip shotgun on April 28. Perez was initially arrested on a second-degree manslaughter accusation. Portland police were called to the home in the 11700 block of East Burnside Street about 2:35 p.m. Caron was found dead, facedown on the living room couch. A 12-gauge pump shotgun was on a loveseat a few feet away. Perez had run out of the house screaming after the shooting. Neighbors heard him yelling, "He's dead! He's dead!" Perez told a neighbor he was holding a shotgun, and it "just slipped," and he had shot his roommate, according to a search warrant affidavit. Perez later told detectives he was on the loveseat, fiddling with the shotgun he had bought a few days earlier, loading and unloading the gun. A single blast struck Chance in the head. Since the shooting, Caron's parents, Lance and Helen Caron, have been supportive of Perez, who was a close friend of their son, and have pledged to help him through the ordeal. Lance Caron said he didn't know his son or Perez had guns or were interested in them. A Multnomah County grand jury, after hearing additional testimony, decided Perez acted with criminal negligence, not recklessness, and reindicted him on a lesser charge of negligent homicide. SOLVED

7. Richard M. Cichon, 18. Stabbed to death early May 22 on Northwest Davis Street between First and Second avenues in Old Town after a fight that started on a MAX train. Police took Nicholas Jordan Amidon, 26, into custody on accusations of murder. A grand jury declined to indict Amidon in the stabbing death. Authorities say Amidon stepped onto a MAX headed for downtown Portland. Cichon and another teen, Alton J. Hardwick, then 19, began arguing with Amidon and fighting with him. Amidon pulled out his knife and stabbed Cichon. Hardwick pleaded guilty to disorderly conduct and was sentenced to three years of probation. Hardwick told police that he and Cichon had been drinking. On June 25, state dismissed Amidon's murder charge, saying it couldn't proceed with prosecution.SOLVED.

Eldon Smith
8. Eldon Jay Rebhan Smith, 4. Drowned in the Willamette River early May 23 after being thrown from the Sellwood Bridge along with his 7-year-old sister, who survived. A Multnomah County grand jury indicted Amanda Jo Stott-Smith, the 31-year-old mother, on charges of aggravated murder and attempted aggravated murder. Stott-Smith is accused of forcing her two young children off the Sellwood Bridge in the middle of the night during her weekend visitation with her son and daughter. She told authorities she committed the crime in an act of revenge against her estranged husband, who obtained temporary custody of the children in April after a dispute that had the children bouncing from three homes in three months. She was arrested about 10:25 a.m. May 23 in a downtown Portland parking garage. SOLVED.

9. Borisshell Washington, 18. A week before her graduation from Jefferson High School, the senior was gunned down about 9:20 p.m. May 31 at the entrance to Raymond Park off Southeast 118th Avenue. Washington was attending a barbecue in the park and died from a gunshot to the head. Police said there were gang associates at the large gathering in the park and Washington was not the intended target. Witnesses and neighbors along Southeast 118th Avenue heard three gunshots. Some saw and heard at least two cars speed away on the unpaved road. Washington was left behind in a dust cloud, collapsing on the dirt entrance to the park. Just days before the shooting, Washington's mother and siblings had moved out of North Portland to a house in outer Southeast Portland to escape violence. Washington, whom friends and family called Shel or Shelly, helped persuade her family to move. The teen had lined up modeling work with a local hair salon and was enrolled in Phagans' School of Hair Design in Northeast Portland. UNSOLVED.

Fernando Chavez
10. Fernando Chavez, 17. Shot in Southeast Portland about 3:30 a.m. June 6 while chasing a man who had tried to pry open his foster mother's bedroom window. Chavez ran 2 1/2 blocks after the stranger, who had grabbed a mountain bike outside Chavez's house before ditching it in a neighbor's yard. At one point, Chavez may have seen a weapon and tried to retreat, but he was shot in the head and collapsed in the middle of Southeast Knapp Street. "He died protecting his family," said Cindy Jenik, his foster mother. The alleged gunman, Thomas Konschuh, 30, who was taken into custody at his sister's home on Knapp Street, faces allegations of murder and attempted aggravated murder. Chavez, a junior at Cleveland High School who had attended Franklin, was in spring training for football. He also worked as a busboy at La Bamba Restaurant. SOLVED.

11. Todd Burton Ternes, 46. Found bludgeoned, with his throat cut, inside a Northeast Portland home off 162nd Avenue the afternoon of July 12. Police said they were notified of the death about 5:30 p.m. when his sister found her brother's body. Ternes lived with his sister and her two sons. Ternes had recently been laid off from a commercial fishing job in Alaska, his sister said, and was considering moving again because he couldn't find work in Portland. He was trying to help her pay the rent, doing yardwork for neighbors. Ternes, born in North Dakota, moved with his family to Portland at age 4. He led a hard life marred by drug and alcohol abuse, leading to criminal convictions from 1988 through 2001 and a restraining order filed against him in 1999 by his father. Police say they don't suspect the killing was a random act of violence. UNSOLVED.

12. Jeisy Sikiwo Moses, 39. Found dead at the east side of the Steel Bridge on the Eastbank Esplanade about 10:30 p.m. on Aug. 3. Michael Adkins, 43, was accused of stabbing Moses after a fight broke out involving several transients, possibly over a woman. Adkins was seen swinging a large knife back-and-forth, trying to seek revenge against whoever struck him in the head with a beer can and others who were throwing rocks. Moses was heard urging Adkins, Lets not use knives, lets use fists, before Adkins is accused of grabbing him and falling with him down an embankment towards the rivers edge. There, witnesses said they heard Moses scream, Ive been stabbed. He suffered 11 stab wounds or lacerations to his body. SOLVED.

13. Gregory Toeniskoetter, 42. Died of head trauma in his home about 10 p.m. Aug. 8. Portland police responded to a welfare check in a condominium complex at 614 S.W. Nevada St. Unit A. Police suspect the killing may have been a drug-related killing. UNSOLVED.

14. Joseph Erzen, 52. Shot while sleeping inside his home at 5709 S.E. Flavel St. on Aug. 22. Investigators suspect the bullets that went through the wall of his bedroom were not intended for Erzen. Homicide detectives aren't sure who may have been the target and are continuing to investigate. Erzen rented a room in a tiny yellow cottage at the corner of Southeast Flavel Street and 57th Avenue. He had worked in local tire shops for more than 30 years. Neighbors heard gunshots about 3 a.m. Bullets had penetrated the south side of the house and struck a vehicle parked alongside the house. Erzen's longtime employer, Ken Hutchens, of Ken's Tire Service, said he was baffled by the crime, and described Erzen as a hard worker and always willing to lend a hand to others. UNSOLVED.

15. Jeanine Toth, 50. Found stabbed to death at 6956 N. Fessenden St. on Sept. 8. Her body was found underneath a blanket on a mattress on the bedroom floor, amid bottles of alcohol. Police found a steak knife at the scene and other evidence. She had been sexually assaulted, and died of multiple stab wounds. UNSOLVED.

16. James Thedrick Lambert, 52, died Sept. 21, eight days after he burglarized a home in the 1300 block of Northeast Sumner Street. He fell down the stairs during a confrontation with the two homeowners and then ran from police. He collapsed when police handcuffed him, and died more than a week later at Legacy Emanuel Hospital & Medical Center from a combination of health conditions exacerbated by the burglary confrontation, according to Dr. Christopher Young, a deputy state medical examiner. Lambert had a heart condition that predisposed him to suffer arrhythmia. His heart stopped pumping blood, resulting in not enough oxygen to the brain. Young determined that the altercation during the home burglary caused him physiologic stress that put him into an arrhythmia. He ruled it a homicide, death at the hands of another person. Lambert was cooperative with police, and there was no struggle with officers. No charges were filed. SOLVED

Kipp Crawford
17. Kipp D. Crawford, 31. Found dead in the intersection of North Willamette Boulevard and North Haven Avenue about 2:30 a.m. Nov. 3. Police suspect someone robbed Crawford in the street, and he was then run over by two motorists. They are considering the death a homicide, suspecting that the unidentified person or person who robbed Crawford contributed to his death by leaving him in the street. The two motorists who struck Crawford were accused of drunken driving. Crawford, a drummer who played in at least three Portland-based bands, died of multiple blunt force trauma to his head and chest. UNSOLVED.

18. and 19. Tameka Medina, 28, and Ashawn Medina-Barr, 4, both found shot in the head Nov. 5 in the Southeast Portland home they once shared with Ashawn's father. Tameka had moved away in an effort to escape from her abusive boyfriend and her children's father. She left her 11-year-old daughter with her mother until she and Ashawn, who was to start preschool, could get settled in South Carolina. But Tameka returned to Oregon for her mother's birthday. And when her former boyfriend, Ceantwaun Barr, called, saying he was sick, Tameka went to his house on Nov. 4 with Ashawn Tameka's mother, Brenda Medina, discovered all three of them dead just before 10 a.m. the next day. Police say Ceantwaun Barr shot Tameka and his son and then shot himself. Tameka Medina had obtained a restraining order against Barr on June 9, 2006, saying he had threatened to kill her, punched and slapped her, and struck her with metal tools. "He is jealous and wants to control me," she wrote in her application for the order. Three days later, Medina asked a judge to dismiss the restraining order. "He has entered counseling for bipolar, and abusing me," she wrote. "Is starting medication. I am moving out of state. He poses no threat to my safety or anyone else." SOLVED.

Nancy Bergeson
20. Nancy Bergeson, 57. Strangled to death in her home Nov. 24. at 4146 S.W. Hamilton.The assistant federal public defender had been found that afternoon lying face down in the dining room of her Southwest Portland home. A girl who stopped by daily to walk her golden retriever saw her through a front window and alerted a neighbor. Portland patrol officers were called about 3 p.m. A police forensic criminalist and a deputy medical examiner went to the home, and the deputy thought it was a natural death. Homicide detectives were not called in until an autopsy was done the following morning. There was no sign of forced entry, and the house wasn't ransacked. The front door was unlocked, but investigators were told that wasn't unusual. UNSOLVED.

21. Amanda Sheppard, 27. Stabbed outside Papa Son's Tavern on Southeast Powell Boulevard on Dec. 5. Kristina Collette Shelton , now 41, accused of aggravated murder in the stabbing death. Sheppard died from a single stab wound to the chest. The two women had been dating the same man, and Dec. 5 was the first time they had met one another. For Shelton, this marked the second time she's been accused of homicide. She was previously convicted of manslaughter in the stabbing death of another woman. SOLVED.

EAST MULTNOMAH COUNTY

1. Gerald Stomps, 60, allegedly was killed by his wife, Hazelynn Stomps, 55. The Corbett woman told police on Friday, Feb. 6 that her husband was missing and she had been assaulted by two unidentified men. She was arrested Thursday, Feb. 12 on suspicion of murder by Multnomah County Sheriff's deputies. Hazelynn Stomps told police she had been pushed off a bridge over Gordon Creek. She said it had taken her two hours to crawl back up to the road, where a passerby found her. She claimed they had been fishing and two strange men started to fight with them and waved a gun. Deputies entered Gerald Stomps into a national database of missing persons. But after several days of searching with dogs and dozens of officers tracking along the Sandy River, investigators were unable to find a trace of him. Inconsistencies in Stomps' story prompted detectives to search the couple's house, where they found evidence that implicated Stomps in her husband's disappearance. She was indicted Monday, Feb. 23, on charges of murder, abuse of a corpse and filing a false police report. SOLVED.


CLACKAMAS COUNTY

1. Joseph Patrick Haley, 28, was shot to death March 20 in a Milwaukie-area apartment complex. The shooting was reported at 10:43 a.m. at the Willow Creek Apartments, 3624 S.E. Westview Road. A woman and another man who were in the apartment were not injured, said Clackamas County Sheriff's Office spokesman Jim Strovink. Investigators are looking for two men, about 6 feet tall and wearing "white puffy coats," believed to be involved in the shooting. UNSOLVED.

2. Bradley Michael Snell, 38, of Milwaukie was shot and killed April 21 by Clackamas County sheriff's snipers after Snell held three hostages at gunpoint for two hours, threatening to shoot them. The shooting immediately secured the safety of Kelly D. Finley, a 37-year-old single mother who was home with her two sons when Snell, a neighbor, pushed his way into her apartment on Southeast 21st Avenue and threatened to kill her unless she arranged for delivery of his medications. While Snell held his .22-caliber semiautomatic pistol on her, Finley frantically called pharmacies and clinics to obtain prescription drugs that Snell took for severe ulcerative colitis. During a lull in the threats, Finley stashed her two sons, ages 8 and 7, in her bathroom. And when Snell stepped outside, members of the Clackamas County special weapons and tactics team shot him. A grand jury found the shooting justified, declining to indict Sgt. Tim Beard and Deputy Justin Brummett. SOLVED.

3.Jesse Aaron Weatherford, 22, of Milwaukie was shot and killed by police June 2 after provoking an armed confrontation at the complex where he lived with his mother. Police were called to the Northridge Condominiums, 12600 S.E. Freeman Way, to investigate a report of a bleeding man with a knife. When officers arrived, Weatherford ignored their orders to drop his knife and stop. When he continued to advance, they shot Weatherford with a less-than-lethal 40 mm sponge round, striking him on the foot. When he ignored further orders, police shot him twice in the chest. Medical examiners called noting that he had tried to kill himself twice in the hours before the confrontation and had called his mother to say goodbye. A Clackamas County grand jury declined to indict Sgt. Tom Broomfield and Officer Luke Strait in the shooting.

4. Jose Ramirez-Navarro, 25, of Southeast Portland was found lying on the sidewalk outside a Clackamas-area apartment complex on July 13, with a severe stab wound to his abdomen. He was rushed to OHSU Hospital in Portland, but later died. A suspect in the stabbing, Ruben Anthony Arteaga, 20, is being held without bail in the Clackamas County Jail, charged with murder, manslaughter and second-degree assault. His trial in Clackamas County Circuit Court is set for Feb. 8, 2010. Arteaga was arrested by Vancouver police three days after the stabbing, which occurred during a street fight involving 10 or 15 people. A Milwaukie man also was stabbed in the melee, but recovered from his injuries.

5. Vivian Faye Bighaus, 66, who lived at East Fernwood Circle in Sandy, died after she was shot by a high-powered rifle around 3:15 p.m. on Christmas Day. Her husband of 44 years, Phillip Bighaus, 67, was present during the shooting, and immediately called 9-1-1. Clackamas County sheriff's deputies shut down a two-mile stretch of U.S. 26 and launched a manhunt for the couple's son, 41-year-old Michael Scott Bighaus. He was arrested the following day, when he returned to his parents' house at 6 a.m. Phillip Bighaus told deputies his son has a mental disorder and may not have been taking his prescribed medications. Michael Scott Bighaus was arraigned Dec. 28 in Clackamas County Circuit Court on a charge of murder. He is scheduled to appear in court for a prelinary hearing on Jan. 5. A trial date has not been set. Meanwhile, he is being held without bail in the Clackamas County Jail.

WASHINGTON COUNTY

1. Nabintu Kelekele Kalamba, 49, of Beaverton was found bludgeoned to death with a hammer early March 16. Police responded to 6155 S.W. King Blvd. about 1 a.m. after getting a call from the victim's 24-year-old daughter who said her mother had been seriously hurt. The daughter was asleep on the first floor of the town home at the time of the assault. The victim was on the second floor and was pronounced dead at the scene. Beaverton police arrested 50-year-old Namegabe Kelekele Mushegero on suspicion he beat his wife to death. Police said they do not have a motive for the crime but that the murder weapon appears to be a hammer. SOLVED.

2. Juanita Rosales-Garcia, 40, disappeared in early March after having an argument with her boyfriend, Delfino Hurtado-Navarette. An Oregon State Police trooper found her body April 3 in a wooded area near North Plains, after searching places where she had used her cell phone. Hurtado-Navarette was arrested April 6 and is being held in the Washington County Jail on one count of murder. Police think Rosales-Garcia told Hurtado-Navarette she was going to leave him, after the couple had been together eight years, said Lt. Michael Rouches, Hillsboro Police Department spokesman. The autopsy found death was due to extensive bleeding and wounds to the throat from a sharp object. Hurtado-Navarette first reported Rosales-Garcia missing on March 10. Hillsboro police discovered Rosales-Garcia's Chevrolet Blazer April 1, abandoned in a local parking lot. Hurtado-Navarette is also sought by the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agency for being in the country illegally, Rouches said. SOLVED.

3. Eluard Corzo Bernal, 21, of Hillsboro, died in the hospital May23, several days after being injected with an overdose of heroin. Investigators originally thought the man had injected himself, but after a three-month investigation, arrested Kaleb Abraham, 21, on accusations of manslaughter. Investigators say Abraham, of Aloha, allegedly administered the heroin to get his friend high. Abraham was a regular user, Corzo Bernal an occasional user. When Corzo Bernal stopped breathing, Abraham used CPR on him and went back to bed, only to be awakened later by other roommates who reported Corzo Bernal was not breathing again. When CPR failed, Abraham drove him to the hospital so police wouldn't come to the house in Aloha and discover drug paraphernalia. Abraham was arrested Oct. 8 on second-degree manslaughter, drug and unrelated DUII charges. SOLVED.

4. Maya Gramajo, 2 months, was unconscious and not breathing when paramedics were summoned to her Aloha home. She had suffered traumatic brain injury and was placed on life support. She died on May 27. Her father, Eber Gramajo-Calderon, 32, was arraigned on murder charges after the infant was declared dead. His trial is scheduled for Feb. 9. SOLVED.

5. and 6. Tyler Gumm, 7, and his 6-year-old sister, Kylie, were shot and killed on May 29 by their adoptive father, James Gumm, 45. Hillsboro police said Gumm shot the children and then himself on a trail at Jackson Bottom Wetlands Preserve outside Hillsboro. Gumm worked as a customer engineer for 20 years at Applied Materials, a Hillsboro semiconductor company, servicing multimillion-dollar equipment. Spokesman David Miller said Gumm "chose to leave the company" in February; he declined to comment on why. SOLVED.

Heather Snively
7. Heather Megan Snively, 21, was found dead on June 5 in the crawl space of a Cedar Hills home. The pregnant woman died of blunt force and cutting injuries. Her fetus had been removed from the womb. Korena Elaine Roberts, who claimed to have given birth to Snively's baby, was arrested on murder charges. Rescuers who responded to a report of an infant in distress noted a lot of blood in the house, at 13380 N.W. Mill Creek Drive, and sent the baby and Roberts to Providence St. Vincent Medical Center. Doctors could not revive the baby and discovered that Roberts had not recently delivered a baby, according to the sheriff's office. Washington County deputies searched the home and found Snively dead of homicidal violence. Roberts is being held without jail pending her trial. SOLVED.


Alexander Hernandez-Apale
8. Alexander Hernandez-Apale, 18, died Sept. 1 of multiple stab wounds after being attacked around 11:40 p.m. the night before on Southwest Hall Boulevard near Colony Lane in Beaverton. Police say he was a random victim who was not involved in gangs. Hernandez was walking with a 19-year-old man when three men pulled up in a car and began asking them what gang the two were affiliated with. One of the men from the car pulled a knife, and the two men tried to run. All three men from the car chased after Hernandez, pushed him to the ground and stabbed him in the chest and upper torso. Police later arrested three men: Duane E. Corbett, 22, and Maciel Munoz, 18, who were captured in Eugene, and Nicholas J. Sias, 23, who police at first thought was the victim of another stabbing the same night. His story, however, didn't add up, according to police, who were led to the other two men. All three face accusations of murder. SOLVED.

Teresa Beiser
9. Teresa Beiser, 36, of Gladstone, was shot by her estranged husband in inside her workplace, Legacy MetroLab-Tualatin shortly before noon on Tuesday, Nov. 11. Two co-workers were wounded. The shooter, her estranged husband, Robert Beiser, 39, was found dead of an apparent self-inflicted gunshot wound. They had two children, a 14-year-old daughter and an 11-year-old son. The shooting was the first killing in this city of 26,000 since August 2001 and just its sixth since 1987. SOLVED.

10. Lori Fitzgerald, 47, found dead Nov. 7 in her home in Forest Grove. Seventeen days later, police said she was the victim of an apparent homicide. Forest Grove arrested Josh David Nicholas, who they initially said was wanted on two outstanding warrants, in a Yamhill County home on Nov. 24. Forest Grove police say Nicholas had recently become acquainted with Fitzgerald and that they suspect he was in her home prior to police finding her body on Nov. 7. Nicholas has not been charged, but he has officially been identified as a person of interest in the murder. Capt. Jeffrey Williams said news of the slaying was not reported to the public because the initial call was of a suicide. However, he said, it became apparent early in the investigation that Fitzgerald's death was more likely a homicide. He said possessions, including guns, were missing from Fitzgerald's home. He spent two days in critical condition at Legacy Emanuel Hospital and died Dec. 1 after Wise's brother, Charles Wise of Texas, told police and hospital officials not to keep his brother on life support.
