  January 4th, 2010 from Bob Buckhorn
LISTEN: Bob Buckhorn talks jobs, panhandling with Jack & Tedd on AM Tampa Bay


Click play to listen!

BOB ON JOBS: “We’ve knocked on over 15,000 doors and everywhere I go on porches all over the community people are concerned about their future and their concerned with their kids futures and they want this election to be about the future. When I’m talking with them they’re talking about economic security and they’re talking about how to get Tampa back on track. We have lost our way. We are not competitive with Charlotte and Austin and Raleigh Durham and all the cities that attract and steal our best and brightest kids and so we’ve got to find a way to maneuver back to that point...”
"...For me, as the father of two little girls, I don’t have a choice and so we’ve jumped into this race and we have a plan. Our plan involves two parts one is internal and external, stripping down that bureaucracy and that regulatory process to make us more business friendly because we are known around the country as a place that is very tough to do business, very tough to pull permits but we can do that. As a strong mayor I have the authority to go in and deal with the regulatory process but, externally, how we position ourselves to the rest of the country and the rest of the world is just as important which means a mayor has to go out on the road and has to be a salesman for his community. We’ve got to identify those strategic advantages that we have whether it’s cyber security, whether it’s financial services, whether its bio-tech. We’ve got to build on the success of USF medical school, M2Gen, Draiper Labs, Moffitt Cancer Center and find a way to spin those research projects. We are the second largest recipient or research dollars in the state of Florida other than Gainesville so we’ve got to find a way to move that from the petri dish to the marketplace and that will create the kinds of jobs that Jackson will come home to and my two girls will come home to but we can’t do it by looking through the rear view mirror. We have got to look ahead of us and to the future so we’re in, we’re all in and we expect to win”.

BOB ON PANHANDLING: “Well Teddy if you recall I had passed an ordinance before I left office that had banned all that. We passed an ordinance that allowed legitimate charities to apply for a permit once a year provided they had the right liability coverage which allowed the Shriners and Fire Fighters and some of the vendors to do what they’ve been doing for years without causing problems. There was no panhandling when I left office but now we’ve seen it explode and we’ve seen unwillingness on the part of elected officials in this community to do anything about it. I hear it all over the city and when I get there we’re going to get the panhandlers off the corners. We’re going to get them out of the intersections but I also understand and I’m tough enough to deal with that problem but tender enough to understand that some of these folks have some serious issues. At the same time we have to remain cognizant that we don’t have enough beds for alcoholism treatment and we don’t have enough bed for drug rehabilitation. We’ve got to find a way to help these people but you don’t do it by enabling deviant behavior and that deviant behavior is a hazard. It’s dangerous and somebody’s bound to get killed and then they’re going to sue the city”.
