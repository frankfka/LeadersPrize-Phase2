Paper

The Economic Consequences of Delay in U.S. Climate Policy

June 3, 2014, Warwick J. McKibbin, Adele Morris and Peter J. Wilcoxen

Warwick McKibbin, Adele Morris and Peter Wilcoxen analyze a number of climate policy scenarios using an economic model of the United States to find that by nearly every measure, delayed policies produce worse economic outcomes than a more modest policy implemented immediately. The authors further find that an immediate carbon tax could significantly strengthen the economy if it were used to reduce distortionary taxation. Read More ›
