    At first, Milwaukee's temperature was taken downtown

    When Milwaukee's first weather bureau opened, Doppler radar hadn't been invented and Mitchell International Airport didn't exist because the Wright brothers were either not born (Orville) or taking those first steps (Wilbur).

    The Weather Channel wasn't around either.

    It's easy to gripe about the weather, especially when forecasters get it wrong, but think back to 1870. The Great Lakes were the equivalent of the interstate highway, and when a gale blew in or winds whipped Lake Michigan or a winter blizzard arrived early, thousands of lives and millions of dollars in commerce were at stake.

Public employee union fights back on Walker claims over benefits

By Lee Bergquist of the Journal Sentinel
Dec. 10, 2010
  • Tweet
  • Email
  • Print
  • (0) Comments

A public employee union countered Walker's claims on Friday that the benefits of government employee are too rich.

AFT-Wisconsin, which represents 17,000 professional public employees, offered the following take in a statement it release a few minutes ago: 

  • State employees have agreed to over $100 million in concessions in this biennium alone.
  • Sate employees did not create the budget deficit, and it cannot be plugged on the backs of state workers alone.
  • According to the Wisconsin Taxpayer Alliance, only 4.2% of "direct general expenditures" go towards administration (staff). That ranks 39th in the nation and is 17% less than the US average.
  • Wisconsin state employees are compensated less than their private sector peers. According to an April 2010 study by UW-M researchers Bender and Heywood, Wisconsin state employees  total compensation was 6% below their peers in the private sector (including pay and benefits.)
  • According to census data, Wisconsin ranks 44th in terms of total number of state employees per capita..
