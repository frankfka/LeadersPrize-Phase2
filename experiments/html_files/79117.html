Death-row inmate David Eugene Johnston dies

September 30, 2010|By Anthony Colarossi, Orlando Sentinel

Long-time Florida death row inmate David Eugene Johnston died while in state custody early this morning, according to state Department of Corrections officials.

He died at 3:40 a.m. "from apparent natural causes," according to a brief statement put out by DOC spokeswoman Jo Ellyn Rackleff. A medical examiner will determine his cause of death.

Under federal patient privacy laws and Florida statutes, "we cannot comment on any particular medical condition, medical treatment, or the medical status of inmate Johnston," Rackleff's statement says.

Johnston, who was 50, had been in Department of Corrections custody since June 1984.

Craig Waters, a Florida Supreme Court spokesman, confirmed a separate document from the Department of Corrections stating Johnston was removed from the death row roster and listing him as "deceased."

He said did not know the circumstances of the inmate's death.

The Florida Commission on Capital Cases also posted the Department of Corrections letter with a picture of Johnston on its website.

Earlier this year, Johnston was scheduled to die by lethal injection before issues about whether he was mentally disabled were brought up.

Two experts had said Johnston was mentally retarded according to law. But two other experts for the state said he was not.

Johnston was convicted in the 1983 murder of Mary Hammond. The 84-year-old woman was found stabbed to death in her Orlando home.

To be considered legally retarded, a defendant must have an IQ of 70 or below and can't perform "adaptive functions," such as holding a job, cooking a meal and balancing a checkbook. Both conditions must have existed before the person was 18.

Since Johnston was 7 years old, he has been tested seven times. He was found to be mentally disabled in three tests -- scores ranging from 57 to 65. Others tests -- with scores of 79 to 83 -- found he was not.
