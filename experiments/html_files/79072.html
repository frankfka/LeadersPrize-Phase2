HOUSE: GOP will take House, Rep. Ryan likely to be Budget Committee chair

  • STEPHANIE JONES stephanie.jones@journaltimes.com

    Stephanie Jones

  • Nov 2, 2010

RACINE COUNTY - Get ready America. Control of the House will soon be in new hands and Rep. Paul Ryan will likely chair the key committee that will help shape the next federal budget.

Republican Ryan of Janesville, who represents Racine County, won re-election Tuesday over Democrat John Heckenlively, according to media reports.

Ryan is not the only Republican victory. Republicans won enough seats to control the U.S. House of Representatives, according to media reports.

Along with that change in power, Ryan will likely become chairman of the House Budget Committee, said George Rasley, who is the communication director for Rep. Jeb Hensarling, R-Texas. Hensarling is the vice ranking member of the committee. Ryan is the ranking member of the budget committee, which is the highest position the minority party holds on the committee.

"He is the most highly respected Republican voice on budget issues," Rasley said of Ryan.

The committee is the first to look at the federal budget after the president presents it and can propose bills to cut spending, the latter of which Ryan said he plans to do immediately if the gets the chairmanship.

As chairman, "you can be the architect of the fiscal plan for the country." Ryan said.

"The current Congress is borrowing and spending and raising taxes. I would keep taxes from increasing, stop spending from dramatically increasing."

As a member of the minority party, Ryan was not able to impact the current budget.

"Discretionary spending went up 84 percent in the last two years," Ryan said.

Before the election, the breakdown was 255 Democrats to 178 Republicans and two vacancies.

But now with the power swap, Ryan will be able to have more influence and plans to reduce the discretionary spending.

"I'll write the budget," Ryan said, if he gets the chairmanship. "It would include a plan to pay down the debt and get the country on the right track fiscally to pre-empt a debt crisis from hitting this country."

Ryan has become nationally known for his "Roadmap for America," a plan which includes Social Security and Medicare reform.

But Heckenlively said even with the possible chairmanship, "I don't really see the roadmap going anywhere." Ryan does not have enough support for it even within his own party, said Heckenlively.
