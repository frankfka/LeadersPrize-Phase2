By DAVE SERENO
The Repository
Posted Jan 03, 2011 @ 11:04 PM

Eddie Lee Sexton Sr., the former Stark County resident who made national headlines in the 1990s amid murder and tales of abuse and incest within his family, has died in a Florida prison.

Sexton, 68, died of apparent natural causes Wednesday in the Union Correctional Institution in Raiford, Fla., where he was on Death Row, prison officials said.

Sexton was found dead in his cell at 5:46 a.m. There were no signs of foul play, officials said.

An autopsy has been completed but a final ruling on the cause is not expected for several weeks pending toxicology and other tests, which are standard for prison deaths, said Larry Bedor, director of investigations for the Florida Medical Examiner’s District 8 office.

Sexton had pre-existing heart disease, Bedor said.

Fleeing Jackson Township

Sexton and his family lived in Jackson Township before fleeing in late 1992 amid allegations he and his wife had abused some of their 12 children.

In November 1992, Sexton had a six-hour standoff with Jackson Township police while demanding he get a new case worker in his ongoing dispute with social workers from the Child Protective Services division of what was then known as the Stark County Department of Human Services. He served six days in jail before being released and disappearing from the area.

While on the run and living in a Tampa, Fla.-area state park, Sexton had his son-in-law, Joel Good, killed in late October or early November 1993, fearing Good was preparing to leave and would disclose the family’s whereabouts to Ohio authorities.

Good, a 24-year-old Jackson High graduate, had married Sexton’s daughter, Estella “Pixie” Good, and was with the family while they were on the lam. The group lived in a motor home in Tampa-area state parks.

Eddie Lee Sexton Sr. is believed to have fathered three children with two of his daughters and ordered his 22-year-old mentally challenged son, Willie Sexton, to strangle Good, according to trial testimony.

The elder Sexton used physical and sexual abuse to control his children, investigators said.

“Each of his children represented a key that would unlock a door to a different secret,” Assistant State Attorney Jay Pruner told jurors in Eddie Lee Sexton Sr.’s first murder trial.

“To keep those family secrets sacred, Eddie Lee Sexton exercised complete domination and control over every member of his family.”

New trial, same result

Sexton Sr. was convicted of aggravated murder and other charges in Hillsborough County, Fla., in November 1994 and sentenced to die.

He was later given a second trial after a higher court ruled that admissions from Sexton’s past unduly influenced jurors in the first trial. He was convicted a second time and again given the death penalty.

Sexton also admitted plotting to kill a disabled camper in hopes of assuming the man’s identity. He was arrested and charged with Good’s death before the plot could be carried out.

Sexton’s wife, Estella Sexton, was convicted in Stark County Common Pleas Court of rape and other charges involving their children. She has been imprisoned since 1996. She has a parole hearing slated for February, according to the Ohio Department of Rehabilitation and Correction.

“Pixie” Sexton-Good was convicted of smothering her infant son, “Skipper,” to quiet his screams while the group was living in their motor home in the Tampa area. She was sentenced to six years in prison.

The bodies of Good and the infant were found in shallow graves in Florida state parks.

The Sextons’ tale was later chronicled in the book “House of Secrets” by Lowell Cauffiel.

“Of all the psychopaths and narcissists I’ve written about in a half dozen true crime books, Eddie Lee Sexton ranks at the top as evil incarnate,” Cauffiel wrote in a message to The Repository on Monday.

“I’m sure a lot of people were looking forward to him taking a ride in Old Sparky, Florida’s electric chair. But like the many years he dodged justice as he committed unspeakable abuse on his family, he seems to have had one last move left in him by having a natural death.”
