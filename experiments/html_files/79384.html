UWM moves forward on Wauwatosa land purchase

  • e-mail
  • print
By Tom Daykin of the Journal Sentinel

Dec. 15, 2010 |(6) Comments

Click to enlarge

A University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee Foundation affiliate has raised the $5 million down payment on a proposed research park site in Wauwatosa and will make the purchase in January.

The group also will seek more time to make a second installment payment on the deal, said Bruce Block, attorney for the UWM group, which is buying the 88-acre tract east of U.S. Highway 45 and north of W. Watertown Plank Road. The land will be the site of Innovation Park, which will include a science research center and privately developed buildings for technology-oriented companies.

The foundation has raised money necessary to make its down payment on the $13.55 million purchase of the county-owned parcel, Block said Wednesday. The County Board this fall granted a 30-day extension of the purchase deadline to Jan. 15.

Along with the down payment, the sale agreement calls for installment payments. The first installment payment of $5 million is due in January 2012.

Block said the foundation wants to extend that deadline to January 2014. That will require County Board approval.

The group wants more time on the second payment "given the continued softness in the economy and the challenges we faced in raising the first $5 million." Block said.

He also said part of that second payment could be made earlier than January 2014 if the foundation is able to sell portions of the site. Block said he expects the historic Eschweiler-designed buildings at the County Grounds site to be sold in 2011 to a developer that would convert them into apartments.

Block said County Board Chairman Lee Holloway "understands the situation and is supportive. He believes it is important that we proceed with the initial payment and the closing, and we agree."

In October, retired business executive Michael Cudahy said he will donate $2 million to the effort. Foundation President David Gilbert also said then that a local foundation, which he declined to name, had pledged another $1 million.

The other donors have requested their names not be disclosed, Tom Luljak, vice chancellor of university relations, said Wednesday.

The news of Cudahy's pledge came just over a year after Cudahy said he would not help fund Innovation Park.

Cudahy said in October he had a change of heart after UWM Chancellor Carlos Santiago resigned and was succeed on an interim basis by Michael Lovell.

Cudahy and Santiago had a high-profile spat last year after UWM dropped its initial plans to locate the headquarters for the School of Freshwater Sciences at the site of the former Pieces of Eight restaurant.

Innovation Park's first development will be a 25,000-square-foot building to house university and business research activities. UWM was awarded a $5.4 million federal grant to develop the building, with construction to begin next spring.

University officials said that building, known as a business accelerator, and other developments at Innovation Park will help UWM strike partnerships with the Medical College of Wisconsin, leading to more research grants and business spinoffs.

The City of Wauwatosa has agreed to pay $12 million for roads, sewers and other public improvements, with those funds to be repaid through property taxes generated by developments at Innovation Park. University-owned buildings will be tax-exempt, but the land and privately owned buildings will generate property taxes.
