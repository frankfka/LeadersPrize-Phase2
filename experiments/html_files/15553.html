Trump Tape To Remain Issue For NY Democrats

From the Morning Memo:

For New York Republicans, the lewd and vulgar comments made by presidential nominee Donald Trump won’t be going away anytime soon.

The state Democratic Committee and Gov. Andrew Cuomo over the weekend stepped up their attacks on New York GOP officials, challenging them to abandon their party’s nominee after he was heard on a 2005 video bragging about being able to do whatever he wanted to women because he is a “star” including grabbing their genitalia.

For Democrats in New York, the tipping point in the Trump tape saga came in part after the state committee advertised a “Women For Trump” rally in an Albany suburb scheduled for Wednesday.

But at the same time, the pushback also came after it was revealed the state GOP committee was helping fund Trump mailers in swing states, which the committee has no intention of pulling away from.

Two statements were released over the weekend from the state committee co-chairs, Byron Brown and Christine Quinn.

And Cuomo himself blasted the comments on NY1 and called on Republicans to do the same.

Republicans like Cox and Rob Astorino have increasingly been a thorn in Cuomo’s side. Astorino, the 2014 nominee for governor, called Cuomo and his counterpart in New Jersey Chris Christie to resign after a former ally to Christie testified both men discussed releasing a fraudulent cover story in the Bridgegate scandal.

While some local Republican leaders have condemned the remarks, there has not been a large-scale defection from the badly damaged nominee.

Still, the scandal has not surprisingly caused a stir in hotly contested down-ballot races where control of the Senate is on the line.

“Sue Serino and the entire New York State Republican Party have been loud and clear in their support for Donald Trump throughout his consistent vulgar and anti-woman behavior,” said former Sen. Terry Gipson, a Democrat running for his old Hudson Valley seat.

“Donald Trump’s recent comments and admission of sexual assault is beyond troubling. This is not the kind of representation we need in the White House, or right here in the Hudson Valley.”

Republican Chris Jacobs, who is running for an open seat in western New York, also condemned the remarks.

“I’m appalled and disgusted at these statements made by Mr. Trump. They are unacceptable and most certainly not presidential,” he said. “There is never any excuse for objectifying or demeaning women.”

His opponent, Democrat Amber Small, said she was “terrified and disgusted.”

“These types of comments have no place in political discussions and they have no place in our community,” she said.

And in the race for governor that’s still two years away, prominent Trump supporter and 2010 nominee Carl Paladino made it clear he’s brushing off the Trump remarks, even as he wonders who leaked the video itself.

“What’d I think of what I heard? I thought I heard some basically locker room type talk that goes on with men, all normal men speak that way, normal women speak that way and have like feelings,” Paladino said. “That’s part of our culture.”
