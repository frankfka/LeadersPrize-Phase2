Coast Guard saves three after boat sinks off Cape Cod

Monday
Jul 20, 2015 at 6:19 PM Jul 20, 2015 at 6:19 PM

By Ethan Genteregenter@capecodonline.com

U.S. Coast Guard crews from Cape Cod rescued three people 150 miles off the coast after their ship sank Monday afternoon, according to a statement from the Coast Guard.

The Scotia Provider, from Yarmouth, Nova Scotia, sent out an emergency signal which was received by the Coast Guard command center in Boston at 12:41 p.m., according to the statement.

The crew of the 54-foot fishing boat found their engine room full of water, and abandoned ship into a life raft. Aircraft from Air Station Cape Cod, multiple fishing vessels, and a Coast Guard rescue ship were sent out to assist the Scotia Provider, according to the statement.

Around 3:10 p.m. the three men were plucked out of the water by a helicopter and taken back to Cape Cod.
