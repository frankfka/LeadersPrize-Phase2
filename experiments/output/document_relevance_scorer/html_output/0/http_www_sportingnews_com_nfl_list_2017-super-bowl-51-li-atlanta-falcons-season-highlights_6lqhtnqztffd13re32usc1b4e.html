10 unforgettable moments from the Falcons’ path to Super Bowl 51

nfl February 2, 2017 12:45pm EST February 2, 2017 12:45pm EST A recap of how the Falcons got to Super Bowl 51.

The Falcons are in their second Super Bowl in franchise history and will play the Patriots on Feb. 5 at NRG Stadium in Houston.

Atlanta won the NFC South and was the No. 2 seed in the NFC playoffs.

MORE: Falcons learned "how to finish" in Week 8 win over Packers

Here’s a recap of the Falcons’ path to Super Bowl 51.

Previous Next
1 10

Week 4: Julio Jones sets records vs. Panthers

The Panthers were rethinking their decision to let Josh Norman walk last spring after Julio Jones torched them with 12 catches for 300 yards and a touchdown in a 48-33 Falcons victory.

The Falcons jumped out to a 31-14 victory and the win was so decisive the Panthers actually released cornerback Bene Benwikere in the days that followed.

Jones easily set the Falcons’ single-game receiving record and was just the sixth player in NFL history with 300 or more receiving yards in a single game.

More importantly, the win over the defending NFC champions signaled to the rest of the league that the Falcons were a legitimate contender. 

Previous Next
2 10

Week 5: Vic Beasley, Tevin Coleman with career games vs. Broncos

Falcons outside linebacker Vic Beasley had 3 1/2 sacks in a 23-16 victory in Denver. Beasley had just four sacks in all of 2015 and the second-year pro looked like the player the Falcons were anticipating when they selected him in the first round of the 2015 draft.

Another second-year player, running back Tevin Coleman, also broke out against the Broncos with four catches for 132 yards and a touchdown to go with 31 rushing yards.  

Previous Next
3 10

Week 8: Mohamed Sanu lifts Falcons over Packers

A controversial 26-24 loss to the Seahawks in Week 6 was followed by a 33-30 loss to the Chargers and some doubted whether the Falcons were for real.  

They began to silence critics by turning the corner with a come-from-behind 33-32 victory over the Packers.

After Green Bay quarterback Aaron Rodgers led the Packers on a go-ahead drive late in the game, Matt Ryan engineered an 11-play, 75-yard drive that ended with an 11-yard touchdown pass to Mohamed Sanu.

After losing consecutive games by a total of five points, Falcons coach Dan Quinn said his team “learned how to finish” in the win over the Packers. 

Previous Next
4 10

Week 9: Avenging earlier loss to the Bucs

The win over Green Bay sparked the Falcons for the second half of the season. The Falcons lost just twice over the final nine weeks and avenged their season-opening loss to the Bucs with a dominant 43-28 victory in Tampa.

Jones was again huge with eight receptions for 111 yards as Ryan threw for 344 yards and four touchdowns.

It was a thorough whipping of the Bucs, who scored two touchdowns in the fourth quarter to make the final score somewhat respectable. 

Previous Next
5 10

Week 12: Taylor Gabriel breaks out against Cardinals

In a season of breakout performances, it was Taylor Gabriel’s turn in a 38-19 win over the Cardinals.

The diminutive third-year receiver caught four passes for 75 yards and two touchdowns and added a 27-yard run on an end-around.

It was the first multi-touchdown game for the Browns castoff, who entered the 2016 season with just one TD. Gabriel finished the regular season with six scores. 

Previous Next
6 10

Week 14: Beasley wreaks havoc on Rams

Just as Beasley did to Broncos quarterback Paxton Lynch in Week 5, he abused Rams rookie Jared Goff in a 42-14 victory. Beasley treated the top pick in the 2016 draft like a rag doll, sacking him three times as he had his way with Rams tackle Rob Havenstein.

He even returned a strip-sack for a touchdown that gave the Falcons a 42-0 lead in the third quarter.

Beasley finished the season with 15 1/2 sacks to lead the NFL. 

Previous Next
7 10

Week 15: Devonta Freeman goes off against 49ers

Falcons running back Devonta Freeman ran for 1,079 yards during the regular season, but only had two games in which he went over 100 rushing yards. The first was a 152-yard effort against the Saints, against whom he added 55 receiving yards.

The second came against the 49ers when he ran for 139 yards and three touchdowns in a 41-13 victory.

Previous Next
8 10

Week 16: Falcons beat Panthers to clinch NFC South

The Falcons completed a season sweep of the Panthers to end Carolina’s three-year run of NFC South titles.

The division-clinching victory was decisive as the Panthers picked off Cam Newton twice and had two sacks of the 2015 NFL MVP.

Ryan threw for 277 yards and two touchdowns and Coleman ran for 90 yards, including a 55-yard touchdown in the fourth quarter to put the game out of reach. 

Previous Next
9 10

Divisional playoff: Falcons cruise past Seahawks

Remember that controversial close call in Week 6? There was no such drama in the divisional playoff game as the Falcons beat the wings off the Seahawks in a 36-20 trouncing at the Georgia Dome.

Ryan threw three touchdowns to three different players — Jones, Coleman and Sanu — and Freeman added a rushing touchdown in the win. The Falcons' maligned defense even scored on a safety as Seahawks quarterback Russell Wilson stumbled in the end zone. 

Previous Next
10 10

NFC championship game: Falcons score at will against Packers

The Packers had absolutely no answer for Ryan or Jones, who lit up Green Bay's injury-riddled defense in a 44-21 win.

The Packers failed to score on their first two possessions despite having the ball deep inside Falcons territory. A missed field goal and fumble kept the Packers off the board as the Falcons built a 31-0 lead early in the third quarter.

Jones, who finished with nine catches for 180 yards and two touchdowns, had the highlight of the game with a punishing 73-yard catch-and-run on Atlanta’s second play from scrimmage in the second half.

The win sent the Falcons to their second Super Bowl in franchise history and first since 1998.
