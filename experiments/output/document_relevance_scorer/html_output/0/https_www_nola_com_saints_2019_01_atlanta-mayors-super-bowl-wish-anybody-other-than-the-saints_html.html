Atlanta mayor’s Super Bowl wish: ‘Anybody other than the Saints’

Posted January 15, 2019 at 9:59 AM

By Carlie Kollath Wells, NOLA.com | The Times-Picayune

Atlanta is hosting the Super Bowl this year and that city’s mayor can’t find anything nice to say about the Saints' chances. Bless her dirty bird little heart.

When asked by a reporter for her team predictions, Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms said, “Just anybody other than the Saints. I know there’s going to be a bounty on my head for saying that. But if it can’t be the Falcons, then hey, as long as it’s not the Saints, then I’m happy.”

“Just anybody other than the Saints”

I spoke with Mayor @keishabottoms on Wednesday at City Hall after she announced @SuperBowlGospel!

Reporter Terrell Thomas of TheseUrbanTimes.com tweeted out a video of his interview with the mayor, which was then reported on by The Atlanta Journal-Constitution and others. Saints fans weren’t happy and took to Twitter overnight.

“Y’all know it was a joke right,” Thomas tweeted Tuesday morning (Jan. 15). “It wasn’t a shot at New Orleans as a whole.”

Bottoms retweeted Thomas’s message Tuesday, adding “exactly."

The Super Bowl is Feb. 3 in Atlanta. The Chiefs and Saints are the current betting favorites.
