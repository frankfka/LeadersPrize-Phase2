Falcons' Devonta Freeman: 'Dodging a bullet' is a distraction, contract isn't

NFL
February 1, 2017 3:14pm EST February 1, 2017 3:13pm EST American Football, News, English, Devonta Freeman, NFL, Atlanta Falcons The Falcons running back provided an honest answer to whether his contract would be a distraction during Super Bowl week.

Jordan Heck Omnisport @jordanheckff

Updated at 3:14 p.m. ET

Updated at 3:14 p.m. ET

Devonta Freeman is set to play the biggest game of his football career on Sunday, and he's not letting anything distract him.

One thing that might have distracted him this week was his agent saying Freeman deserved to be paid like an "elite back." Being asked all week about his contract status won't trip up the Falcons running back, though, as he's been through plenty in life.

MORE:Super Bowl 51: Picks, predictions, odds for Patriots-Falcons

Where I come from, where I grew up at, you know I could be playing in the middle of the field we called it The O in the Pork n Beans projects [in Miami] and a shootout might start, Freeman told reporters Wednesday. You know, thats a distraction. Dodging a bullet or something like that, thats a distraction. Thats frustration, going to sleep every night thinking you might hear gun shots. That was the stuff that was distracting to me. This is football. Aint no distraction.

As for the contract, Freeman told NFL.com he does want an extension as he enters the final year of his rookie contract, but he'll deal with that once the season is over. Right now, he's focused on one thing: beating the Patriots in Super Bowl 51.
